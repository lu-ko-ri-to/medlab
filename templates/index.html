About Us

Mater Misericordiae Hospital was opened in 1962 by the Sisters of Mercy, a Catholic Order of Nuns originating from Ireland, three years after registering themselves as the Registered Trustees of an entity under the Perpetual Succession Act (the succeeding legislation after independence).The Hospital in accordance with the philosophy of the Sisters of Mercy aims to reach out to all members of the community irrespective of race, colour or creed, to continue the healing ministry of the church and preserve the heritage of the Sisters of Mercy. As a non-profit institution the hospital seeks to provide the most modern facilities and health care to satisfy the needs of all the community by re-investing any surplus into the continuing mission of the Sisters of Mercy.
